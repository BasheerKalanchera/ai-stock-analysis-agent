# FMCG Valuation Skill

## Core Financials (From Excel)
- **P/E vs Historical Average**: 40-60x is typical for quality FMCG. Growth consistency justifies the defensive premium.
- **Sales CAGR vs OPM Spread**: A proxy for volume vs pricing power. True FMCG compounders show steady sales growth *without* sacrificing OPM.
- **EBITDA / OPM (%)**: > 18% is strong; > 25% is brand premium territory.
- **ROCE (%)**: > 30% expected for asset-light, brand-driven consumer businesses.
- **Receivables Days / Cash Conversion Cycle**: Should be extremely low (or negative). Distributors pay upfront.
- **CFO/PAT Conversion**: > 90% expected.

## Strategy & Narrative KPIs (From PPT)
- **New Product Launches / Category Expansion**: Narrative on expanding the Total Addressable Market.
- **Distribution Reach**: Mentions of rural penetration, direct outlet expansion, or D2C/E-commerce growth.

## Valuation Rules
- FMCG **deserves premium PE** due to brand moats, predictable cash flows, and defensive nature.
- PE below 35x for a market leader with steady growth = "Undervalued" (rare and attractive).
- Compare gross margin trends: raw material inflation hitting margins is often temporary if the brand is strong.

## Governance Check
- FMCG promoters rarely pledge; any pledging > 0% is a RED FLAG.
- Pay attention to Royalty Payments to MNC parents (from Excel/PPT) - rising royalties dilute minority shareholders.

## Secondary / Deep Research Checks (Check filings if needed)
- Exact Volume Growth % vs Price Mix % (usually only found in earnings call or detailed filings).
