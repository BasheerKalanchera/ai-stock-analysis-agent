# Steel & Commodities Valuation Skill

## Core Financials (From Excel)
- **Price-to-Book (P/B) Ratio**: 1.0-1.5x is peak cycle; < 0.6x usually indicates deep value or severe distress.
- **EV/EBITDA**: 5-8x is normal; extremely low EV/EBITDA (2-3x) means earnings have peaked.
- **EBITDA / OPM (%)**: Extremely volatile; look at the directional trend. Spread expansion = Buy.
- **Debt-to-Equity / Absolute Debt**: Rapidly declining absolute debt during upcycles creates massive equity value.
- **CFO/PAT Conversion**: Highly cash generative at peak cycles, funding rapid deleveraging.

## Strategy & Narrative KPIs (From PPT)
- **Capacity Expansion Pipeline**: Phased commissioning of new Mtpa (Million tonnes per annum).
- **Value-Added Mix**: Increasing share of VAP (Value Added Products) like cold-rolled or coated steel vs standard hot-rolled commodities provides margin resilience.
- **Cost Curves / Captive Integration**: Narrative around raw material self-sufficiency (captive iron ore/coal) protecting margins.

## Valuation Rules
- **PE is dangerously misleading** for commodities. High PE often marks the bottom (buy signal), and low PE marks the peak (sell signal). Always use P/B and EV/EBITDA.
- The highest alpha is generated when a heavily indebted commodity player uses peak cycle cash flows to wipe out debt.
- Global macros (China steel exports, global HRC pricing) dictate local pricing, trumping company-specific metrics.

## Governance Check
- Capital misallocation into non-core businesses during upcycles destroys wealth.
- Promoter pledging > 10% is dangerous given earnings volatility.

## Secondary / Deep Research Checks (Check filings if needed)
- Exact EBITDA per Tonne metrics or raw material capacity utilization.
