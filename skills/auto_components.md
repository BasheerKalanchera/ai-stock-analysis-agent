# Auto Components Valuation Skill

## Core Financials (From Excel)
- **P/E vs Peers**: 15-30x depending on growth profile. High-tech/EV components get >30x.
- **EBITDA / OPM (%)**: > 12% is strong; > 15% indicates proprietary/value-add products rather than commoditized parts.
- **ROCE (%)**: > 18% is strong; component makers must show high capital efficiency.
- **Debt-to-Equity**: < 0.5 preferred; sector is naturally capital intensive but leverage should be contained.
- **Receivables Days Trend / CFO Conversion**: Rising receivables or low CFO/PAT (<70%) indicates OEMs are squeezing the component maker on payment terms.

## Strategy & Narrative KPIs (From PPT)
- **Order Book / Revenue Visibility**: Commentary on new platform wins, EV orders, and multi-year contracts.
- **Content Per Vehicle (CPV) Growth**: Narrative on adding more value/content to newer vehicle models.

## Valuation Rules
- **P/E is the primary metric**, adjusted for growth visibility from order book.
- EV content opportunity: companies with EV-compatible products (e.g., electronics, lightweighting, BMS) deserve structural premium over legacy ICE parts makers.
- High OPM stability indicates formula-based raw material pass-through pricing (lower risk).

## Governance Check
- Promoter pledging < 3% acceptable.

## Secondary / Deep Research Checks (Check filings if needed)
- Exact Client Concentration % (Top 3 clients) and Export Revenue % splits.
