# Power Equipment & Capital Goods Valuation Skill

## Core Financials (From Excel)
- **P/E vs Historical Average**: 30-50x during upcycles. Often looks absurdly high during downcycles (denominator effect).
- **Sales CAGR / Order Execution**: Strong topline execution proves backlogs are translating to revenue.
- **EBITDA / OPM (%)**: > 12% is strong; > 15% demonstrates extreme pricing leverage in a booming capex cycle.
- **ROCE (%)**: > 18% is excellent. Peak cycle ROCEs often exceed 25%.
- **Debt-to-Equity**: Must be < 1.0. Heavy debt breaks capital goods companies when the cycle turns.
- **CFO/PAT Conversion**: Focus on Working Capital trends. Slower CFO conversion (<70%) warns of rising retention money or stretched debtor cycles.

## Strategy & Narrative KPIs (From PPT)
- **Order Book / Backlog**: Absolute backlog size vs annual revenue (e.g., 2.5x revenue provides immense visibility).
- **Order Inflow Growth**: YoY growth in new order inflows. If inflows decelerate, the PE multiple will compress violently even if earnings are high.
- **MNC Parentage**: Tech-transfer, royalities, and R&D support from global parents (ABB, Siemens) command a steep institutional premium.

## Valuation Rules
- PE ratios are forward-looking based on Order Inflows. The highest PEs occur at the beginning of the capex cycle.
- Margin expansion (operating leverage) on a fixed asset base drives massive earnings surprises.
- Stretched working capital (rising receivables + inventory) is a leading indicator of execution delays or client weakness.

## Governance Check
- Promoter pledging > 5% is a RED FLAG in cyclical sectors.
- Watch contingent liabilities (bank guarantees).

## Secondary / Deep Research Checks (Check filings if needed)
- Exact Working Capital Days, precise Book-to-Bill Ratio calculations, and segment margins.
