# Healthcare & Hospitals Valuation Skill

## Core Financials (From Excel)
- **EV/EBITDA**: 15-25x is fair for mature hospital chains. High growth justifies upper end.
- **EBITDA / OPM (%)**: > 18% is strong for mature hospitals; new hospitals/greenfield expansions initially pull this down.
- **ROCE (%)**: > 15%. Hospitals are highly capital intensive. 
- **Debt-to-Equity**: Must be carefully managed. Debt-funded capacity expansion is standard, but < 1.0 is preferred.
- **CFO/PAT Conversion**: High predictability. Cash flow should closely mirror PAT.

## Strategy & Narrative KPIs (From PPT)
- **ARPOB (Average Revenue Per Occupied Bed)**: Rising ARPOB is the core lever for revenue growth, indicating better case mix and pricing.
- **Bed Addition / Capacity Pipeline**: Future growth visibility is almost entirely dependent on brownfield/greenfield bed expansions.
- **Occupancy Rate**: > 65% is optimal; > 75% indicates need for new capacity.

## Valuation Rules
- **EV/EBITDA is preferred** over P/E due to massive differences in depreciation and leverage across hospital chains.
- Asset-lite models (O&M contracts) deserve a structural premium to capital-heavy greenfield owners.
- Rising ARPOB + Stable Occupancy + Debt Reduction = Premium Re-rating.

## Governance Check
- Promoter pledging < 5% acceptable if historically standard.
- Track related party land/building leases.

## Secondary / Deep Research Checks (Check filings if needed)
- Exact Payor Mix % (Insurance vs Out-of-Pocket) and precise ALOS (Average Length of Stay).
