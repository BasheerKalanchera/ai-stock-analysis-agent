# Banking & Financial Services Valuation Skill

## Core Financials (From Excel)
- **Price-to-Book (P/B)**: < 1.5 is undervalued for quality lenders; < 1.0 may indicate systemic asset stress.
- **Return on Assets (ROA)**: > 1% is strong for banks; > 2% is strong for NBFCs.
- **Return on Equity (ROE)**: > 15% justifies a P/B premium.
- **Gross NPA (GNPA) & Net NPA (NNPA)**: < 2% GNPA is a clean book; > 5% is a RED FLAG. < 1% NNPA shows excellent provisioning.
- **Capital Adequacy Ratio (CAR) / Tier-1**: > 15% provides headroom for aggressive loan growth.

## Strategy & Narrative KPIs (From PPT)
- **Net Interest Margin (NIM) Trend**: Expanding or stable NIMs > 3% are preferred. Declining NIMs + rising NPAs = SELL.
- **Deposit Franchise (CASA Ratio)**: > 40% indicates a strong low-cost deposit moat (critical for Banks, N/A for NBFCs).
- **AUM Growth / Credit Growth**: Must outpace system growth (>15%) to justify premium.

## Valuation Rules
- **Primary metric is P/B**, not P/E. High ROA/ROE drives P/B expansion.
- Compare CAR headroom: lenders with high CAR can grow without dilutive equity raises.

## Governance Check
- Promoter pledging > 0% in lending is **extremely unusual** and a severe governance red flag.

## Secondary / Deep Research Checks (Check filings if needed)
- Exact Credit Costs % and specific sectoral loan exposure risks.
